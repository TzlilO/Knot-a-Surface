Abstract

Overview

We present Knot-a-Surface, a method for real-time reconstruction of battlefield scenes from a drone swarm under a fixed Gaussian budget. Rather than optimising splat positions freely in 3D space, we ground the reconstruction on a learnable NURBS bicubic control grid — a structured surface that naturally encodes resolution as a knot: tightening the parametric domain over any region of interest concentrates the full splat budget there without retraining.

Coverage weights from swarm view frustums drive an online optimization loop with Laplacian regularization. Adaptive sampling (inverse-CDF warp over curvature, texture, and residual error) concentrates density where reconstruction error is highest; a commander-centric window prioritises the operator's field of view. The result is a reconstructor a field commander can steer live — trading breadth for resolution, committing budget to a zone, and querying terrain with ruler, line-of-sight, and MGRS-style grid references.

Method

Technical Contributions

⟁

NURBS-Grounded Splatting

Gaussian positions are derived from a learnable bicubic control grid with switchable basis — Catmull-Rom (default, interpolating), B-spline degree 2–5, or NURBS rational — over a user-adjustable UV control net. Resolution is a runtime parameter: subdivide the crop to zoom without reallocating the budget.

🛰

Swarm-Aware Optimization

Coverage weights integrate swarm footprint overlap over a strided ≤ 64/axis grid. The time-based decay rate with photometric and Laplacian terms is fps-independent, stable across changing swarm formations and partial occlusion.

◈

Adaptive Budget Allocation

Inverse-CDF warp over a joint curvature + texture + residual importance map concentrates UV samples where error is highest. Fluid mode adds a particle-in-potential-field stage for continuous reallocation as terrain features evolve.

◉

Commander Toolkit

Ray-marched ruler and line-of-sight against the live surface. MGRS-style TACREF grid with per-cell reconstruction confidence, copy-to-clipboard. Free-flying commander camera — the reconstruction re-optimizes wherever it looks, with a pitch-linked world inset.

🛩

Observer-Drone POV

A virtual observer entity flies an autonomous path over the scene as the main display. Its gaze drives the frustum window and region of interest, so the fixed splat budget flows across the terrain beneath it — the surface materializes exactly where the drone looks.

⬚

Segmentation from the Surface

Because the reconstruction is one parametric surface, its RGB / depth / normal field is a flat 2D image indexed by UV. A couple of positive/negative clicks feed a classical region-growing mask that lifts straight back to the 3D Gaussians — revertible isolation, no volumetric labeling. 3D segmentation for the price of a 2D click.

∇

Terrain Gradient Readout

A live slope-shaded map of the fit surface with a downhill quiver (−∇h), read directly from the spline control grid. Surfaces the ridges, drainages, and traversability of the reconstructed terrain at a glance.

Environments

Scene Library

Four procedural battlefield environments. Each is defined by a deterministic h(x,z,t) occupancy and col(x,z,h,t) albedo — any object visible in 3D is also visible to the reconstruction by construction.

🌲
Forest FOB
Treeline with instanced trunk/canopy geometry, forward operating base structures, patrol trajectory.
🏙
Urban
City block with procedural SITE_BOXES building grid — tests wall-scan + facade splat reconstruction.
🌊
Ocean
Animated wave surface with temporal occupancy — tests time-based optimization on non-static terrain.
🏜
Dust
Desert terrain with DUST_TROOPS patrol trajectories and reduced-visibility fbm noise field.
