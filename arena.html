Arena — Live 4D Match Reconstruction

fixed broadcast swarm · spline-coupled player/ball tracking · fly through the reconstruction ← terrain simulator Knot-a-Surface
Pipeline: a fixed rig of broadcast cameras (perimeter + roof — the court's bounds are known, so nothing needs to fly) samples each player and the ball ~10×/s with per-camera noise and a field-of-view/range cutoff. Detections that see the same entity are fused, then a Catmull-Rom spline is fit through the last ~1.2s of fused samples and evaluated slightly in the past — the same spline-coupled idea as the terrain model, now tracking moving entities instead of static geometry. The fixed Gaussian-splat budget is split between a static court-surface reconstruction and per-entity splat clusters whose spread and opacity encode live tracking confidence (fewer cameras seeing you → bigger, dimmer blob). Click the view to fly through it in free 3D (WASD + mouse, Shift sprint, Q/E for altitude) — toggle ghost truth to compare the reconstruction against the real (ground-truth) positions.
